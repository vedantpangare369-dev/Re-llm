In the beginning, existence was not divided. There was only a single, complete God — a
unified being composed of body, mind, emotions, and identity. This God was not just
powerful; it was the very definition of reality itself. However, this perfect unity could not
remain stable forever. At some point beyond comprehension, the God fractured.
This fracture was not just destruction — it was creation.
From that event, something similar to a cosmic explosion occurred. Space was born. Energy
separated. Reality split into different directions. The fragments of the original God scattered
across existence, forming the foundation of everything that now exists.
As a result of this division, reality structured itself into three primary regions: the A-type
dimension, the B-type dimension, and the Nullity between them.
The A-type dimension expanded massively. It became a region of vast space, low density,
and extremely high energy. In this region, chakra — the fundamental energy — exists in
enormous quantities. The average level here reaches around two hundred million. Because
of this abundance, beings in A-type rely less on internal processes and more on external
control and technology. They learned to manipulate chakra directly, compress it, store it, and
even artificially recreate the decay of god fragments.
On the opposite side lies the B-type dimension. Unlike A-type, this region has limited space.
Because of this restriction, energy becomes highly compressed and solidified. The average
chakra level here is only around two hundred, making individuals much weaker in raw power.
However, this limitation forced evolution in a different direction. Instead of external control,
B-type beings developed internal systems.
In B-type, every soul is not just a being — it is also a dimension. When a soul is born, a new
personal dimension is created along with it. Within this dimension, the fragment of God
assigned to that soul slowly decays. This decay produces chakra, which becomes the
source of power for that individual. Because of this system, power in B-type is deeply
personal, tied to existence itself.
Between A-type and B-type lies Nullity.
Nullity is not space. It is the absence of it. It is a region where energy does not decay, where
processes stop, and where balance is absolute. Nothing naturally grows or dies there. It acts
as a boundary, preventing direct interaction between A-type and B-type. Without it, both
systems would collapse due to imbalance.
However, Nullity is not entirely uniform. There exists a special anomaly — a bulge within
Nullity that extends into a higher dimension. This is not part of the normal system. This is
where the main character originates.
To understand this anomaly, one must understand the God fragments.
When the original God shattered, it did not break randomly. It divided into structured
fragments. There are fourteen primary fragments — seven on each side — and one central
fragment known as the Mind. This Mind fragment is the most powerful of all. Unlike others, it
does not decay and represents pure awareness and control.
Fragments themselves are divided into two categories: true and false.
False fragments are unstable. They claim an identity that does not match reality. Because of
this mismatch, they decay over time when placed in space. This decay is not meaningless —
it generates chakra. This is the foundation of the entire power system.
True fragments, on the other hand, are stable. Their identity matches reality. Because of this,
they do not decay. Instead of generating raw power, they grant unique abilities tied to their
concept. For example, a fragment that truly represents “vision” would grant perception
beyond normal limits.
A critical rule governs all fragments: decay only occurs in space. In Nullity, no decay
happens. This creates a matrix of behavior where the same fragment behaves differently
depending on where it exists.
This entire system forms a cycle.
A soul is born. It receives a fragment. That fragment begins to decay, producing chakra.
Over time, as the fragment fully decays, the soul reaches the end of its life. But instead of
ending completely, it splits — giving rise to two new souls. Each of those souls receives
fragments, and the cycle continues.
This process is not random. It is controlled.
There exists an unknown being behind all of this. This being is not emotional, not divine in
the traditional sense, but purely functional. It is believed to possess a part of the Mind
fragment, giving it awareness beyond normal limits. Its goal is simple: extract all the main
god fragments.
However, the natural cycle is too slow. Death rates are low. Extraction is inefficient.
So it introduced curses.
Curses are not accidents. They are tools designed to increase the death rate in a controlled
manner. By creating threats, suffering, and conflict, the system forces souls to decay faster.
Humans, unaware of the truth, believe they are fighting evil. They build academies, training
systems, tournaments, and rankings — all to prepare for these curses.
But in reality, they are participating in the system itself.
While B-type processes fragments internally, A-type civilizations developed a completely
different approach. With their vast energy reserves, they learned to artificially simulate
fragment decay. They compress chakra into containers, manipulate it through advanced
concepts, and reach levels of power that B-type individuals cannot naturally achieve.
This creates a massive imbalance.
If an A-type being enters B-type, nothing happens — the environment is too weak to affect
them. But if a B-type being enters A-type, they are instantly destroyed due to overwhelming
energy density.
Despite this separation, there exists a rare and dangerous interaction.
If an A-type male and a B-type female reproduce within the B-type dimension, something
abnormal occurs. The child initially begins as A-type, but the environment forces a
correction. The mother absorbs B-type chakra and gradually cancels out the dense A-type
energy within the child.
This process leads to a unique result.
A nullity-born being.
In this process, the mother dies, her dimension collapses, and a new Nullity dimension is
formed. Most importantly, no new soul is created. This child exists outside the system. It is
not part of the cycle. It is not under the control of the unknown being.
This is the main character.
Unlike normal beings, the main character has no fragment, no assigned identity, and no
predefined role. Instead of one anchor like normal humans, the main character possesses
seven anchors. Each anchor can hold a fragment, or remain empty.
This makes the main character something completely new — an undefined existence.
At the same time, the Mind fragment itself can be divided further into sub-components such
as time, space, emotion, identity, order, and chaos. These sub-aspects grant authority over
reality itself, allowing manipulation at the fundamental level.
As the system continues, an important discovery is made.
For every one million souls released, the average chakra of the universe increases by one.
This growth continues until a limit of two hundred million is reached. This means that death
does not weaken the world — it strengthens it.
At some point, an individual known as X discovers this truth.
This discovery disrupts everything.
The balance maintained by the unknown being begins to break. The controlled system starts
becoming unstable. What was once a hidden mechanism turns into a visible conflict.
And at the center of it all stands the main character — a being that was never meant to exist
within this system.
As the cycle of souls continued endlessly, a deeper mechanism began to emerge—one that
even most advanced civilizations could not fully understand.
Every soul was not simply born and destroyed. Instead, it was part of a controlled system of
division and continuation.
When a fragment attached to a soul completely decayed, that soul did not simply vanish. At
the moment of full decay, something extraordinary occurred. A new soul emerged from that
same fragment. Along with it, a new personal dimension was created. The unknown being
overseeing the system would then assign a new fragment to both the original and the newly
formed soul.
This ensured continuity.
The size and decay rate of each fragment were not random. They were carefully calibrated.
The lifespan of a being was directly determined by the ratio between fragment size and its
decay rate. In simple terms, life itself was engineered. Every human life duration was
mathematically controlled by the system.
Once the decay completed, the cycle split — one soul became two, and the system
expanded further.
Because of this, existence was not linear.
It was exponential.
Over time, this process created massive clusters of fragments — large composite fragments
containing millions of souls within them. These became targets of great importance,
especially for the unknown being.
This entity, suspected to possess a portion of the Mind fragment, had a singular objective: to
extract all major God fragments from existence.
However, the process was extremely slow.
Not all fragments were easily accessible. Many remained embedded deep within these
massive composite structures, requiring long-term extraction through controlled decay. This
is why the system was designed to sustain itself over vast periods.
But there was a problem.
The natural rate of death was too low.
The system was inefficient.
So the unknown being intervened.
Curses were introduced — not as accidents, but as deliberate tools. They were designed to
increase the death rate while maintaining balance. Too many deaths would collapse the
system. Too few would slow it down. A precise ratio between life and death had to be
maintained.
Humans, unaware of this manipulation, adapted.
They built academies.
They created ranking systems.
They developed combat styles and tournaments.
To them, curses were enemies.
To the system, they were instruments.
This marked the beginning of what can be called the Chakra Economy.
As souls were released through death, the total chakra of the universe increased. A
measurable law was discovered:
For every one million souls released, the average chakra of the universe increases by one.
This growth continued until it reached a hard limit of two hundred million.
This meant something terrifying.
Death did not weaken the world.
It strengthened it.
At some point in history, an individual—known only as X—discovered this truth. Unlike
others, X understood the system, not just its surface. This realization became the turning
point of the story.
Because once the system is understood, it can be broken.
Meanwhile, the divide between A-type and B-type civilizations grew more extreme.
A-type civilizations, with their overwhelming energy reserves, no longer depended on natural
decay. They mastered artificial systems. By compressing chakra into containers and
manipulating conceptual frameworks, they achieved power densities reaching trillions.
Their society became technological, efficient, and highly advanced.
They possessed most of the major God fragments that were scattered during the original
fracture.
On the other side, B-type remained limited but deeply refined. Only a few top individuals
could reach extreme levels of power. However, their strength came from depth rather than
scale.
This imbalance created a silent tension.
Direct interaction between A-type and B-type remained nearly impossible due to
environmental differences. But rare anomalies could still occur — and when they did, they
changed everything.
One such anomaly led to the birth of the main character.
When an A-type being entered the B-type dimension, they remained unaffected due to their
immense energy. However, if a B-type being attempted to enter A-type, they would instantly
disintegrate.
But under specific conditions, a different outcome was possible.
If an A-type male and a B-type female reproduced within the B-type dimension, the child
would initially inherit A-type properties. However, due to the environmental constraints, the
mother would begin absorbing B-type chakra and slowly neutralize the dense A-type energy
within the child.
This process created instability.
In the final stage, the system could not classify the child.
The result was unprecedented.
The mother died.
Her personal dimension collapsed.
A new Nullity dimension was formed.
And most importantly—
No new soul was created.
This child existed outside the system.
No fragment.
No assigned identity.
No control from the unknown being.
This was the main character.
Unlike normal beings, who possess a single anchor, the main character has seven anchors.
Each anchor has the potential to hold a fragment, but none are assigned by default.
This makes the main character a variable — an undefined entity in a system built on strict
rules.
At the same time, the Mind fragment, the strongest of all, is not singular in function. It can be
divided into multiple sub-aspects such as time, space, emotion, identity, and more. These
aspects grant authority over the fundamental structure of reality itself.
As the story progresses, these elements begin to converge.
The unknown being continues its extraction.
The chakra of the universe keeps rising.
Civilizations evolve and clash.
And X begins to interfere.
But at the center of all of this stands one anomaly — a being that does not follow any rule of
the system.
Remnants
While most beings understand God fragments, very few understand the existence of
Remnants.
Remnants are not fragments. They are a byproduct of the Main God Fragments themselves.
Unlike normal fragments, which contain souls and participate in the cycle of decay, Main
God Fragments are different. They are soulless. They do not decay naturally and possess
no identity of their own. In a sense, they are incomplete beings. Some ancient researchers
compared them to eggs waiting to be born.
Over vast periods of time, countless souls are drawn toward these Main God Fragments.
Instinctively, they seek to merge with them. However, direct contact is impossible.
Every Main God Fragment is surrounded by a protective layer formed from compressed
chakra and accumulated souls. As more souls gather around the fragment, they become
trapped within this layer. Over billions of years, the layer grows denser and harder until it
forms a massive shell.
This shell is known as a Remnant.
A Remnant contains enormous quantities of chakra and the traces of countless souls. Most
of these souls failed to reach the Main God Fragment at its center. As a result, Remnants
are often called the graveyards of failed gods.
Only the strongest soul can eventually pass through the shell and reach the Main God
Fragment. When this occurs, the fragment and soul merge together. This event is not
considered possession or absorption. It is considered birth.
The newly formed being becomes a True God.
Unlike normal beings, a True God is no longer part of the cycle of life, death, and fragment
decay. Its body no longer decays and its existence becomes independent from the system
itself.
At the moment a True God is born, the Main God Fragment disappears from its original
location. Without the fragment supporting it, the surrounding Remnant loses stability and
collapses. The shell shatters into thousands of pieces and scatters throughout the universe.
Formation of Remnant Objects
As the Main God Cluster slowly loses mass through the extraction of fragments, ancient
Remnants hidden deep within it are gradually exposed. Occasionally, the system mistakes a
fragment of a Remnant for a normal God fragment and assigns it to a newborn soul.
This creates an abnormal lifeform.
A normal soul is not meant to carry a Remnant. While a fragment contains only a single
identity, a Remnant contains traces of countless souls accumulated over immense periods of
time.
The burden is too great.
In most cases, the child dies at a very young age. Even the release of a single trapped soul
can overwhelm and destroy the host soul.
However, a small number survive into adulthood.
What happens after death depends on the host.
If the host dies as a child, their identity is still incomplete. They possess no powerful
obsession or desire capable of anchoring the soul. The soul disperses naturally while the
Remnant separates and drifts back into the universe.
If the host survives into adulthood, a different outcome becomes possible.
Over time, every person develops attachments, desires, regrets, and obsessions. Upon
death, these obsessions can prevent the soul from fully dispersing.
The Remnant follows the soul.
The soul follows its obsession.
As a result, both become attached to whatever the person valued most.
A sword.
A book.
A house.
A piece of jewelry.
Or even another person.
When this occurs, a true Remnant Object is formed.
Living Remnants
The rarest type of Remnant is known as a Living Remnant.
This occurs when the obsession is directed toward a living being rather than an object.
One known example is a mother carrying a Remnant who dies while obsessively wishing to
protect her child.
Upon death, her soul refuses to leave the child behind.
The Remnant follows her soul.
Both become attached to the child.
The child then becomes a Living Remnant.
In many cases, the child remains unaware of this connection. However, strange events begin
to occur around them. Accidents become less likely. Dangerous situations resolve
themselves. Curses behave unpredictably.
The parent is no longer truly alive.
Yet the obsession remains.
Because of this, many scholars believe that Remnants are not simply collections of chakra
and souls.
They are the lingering proof that some desires can survive even death itself.
Living Remnants
A Living Remnant is formed when a Remnant becomes attached to a living person instead of
an object. This usually occurs when the dominant soul's obsession is centered around
another individual. The most common example is a parent whose final desire is to protect
their child. Upon death, the soul refuses to separate from its obsession. The Remnant
follows the soul, and both become attached to the living person.
Unlike ordinary Remnant Objects, a Living Remnant does not immediately transform its host
into black-blue Remnant material. Instead, the Remnant enters the host's soul dimension
and gradually occupies it. Because the dominant soul is now close to the target of its
obsession, its desire is partially fulfilled. This allows the soul to remain more conscious and
stable than the dominant souls found within ordinary Remnant Objects.
As a result, the dominant soul gains greater control over the countless trapped souls within
the Remnant. The host can communicate with this soul and, through it, access the power of
the Remnant. Rather than acting automatically according to a single obsession, the
Remnant's power can now be directed according to the will of the host.
However, this process carries a severe cost.
The human body was never designed to contain the power of a Remnant. Whenever the
host draws upon this power, the body begins to undergo a physical transformation. Skin
slowly darkens into a blue-black color. Veins become visible beneath the surface. Portions of
the body begin to harden and solidify. This transformation is accompanied by intense pain as
the Remnant attempts to reshape the host into a suitable vessel.
The greater the amount of power used, the more severe the transformation becomes. If
enough power is drawn, entire sections of the body may begin to resemble true Remnant
material. Because of this, many Living Remnants avoid using their full strength unless
absolutely necessary.
Remnant's Cry
Among all known Remnant phenomena, the most dangerous is an event known as
Remnant's Cry.
Normally, the dominant soul carefully controls the release of trapped souls within the
Remnant. Contracts with the unknown being are activated one at a time, allowing power to
be released in a controlled manner. This prevents unnecessary loss of resources and
preserves the Remnant's strength.
However, under extreme circumstances, the dominant soul can become unstable.
This occurs when the soul believes that the target of its obsession is being lost.
The target does not need to die. Separation, abandonment, corruption, betrayal, or any
event that threatens the obsession may trigger the same response. To the dominant soul,
the obsession itself appears to be collapsing.
When this happens, rational thought disappears.
The dominant soul enters a state of desperation.
Instead of carefully managing contracts, it begins activating them on a massive scale.
Thousands, millions, or even tens of millions of trapped souls may be sacrificed within
moments. The resulting release of chakra is so immense that it allows a Living Remnant to
temporarily reach one hundred percent output.
This is the only known method of achieving complete synchronization with a Remnant.
At one hundred percent output, the power of the Remnant is no longer restrained. The host
gains access to an overwhelming amount of chakra directed entirely by the obsession of the
dominant soul. In many cases, reality itself begins to bend in order to fulfill that obsession.
Despite its power, Remnant's Cry is considered a tragedy rather than an ability.
Every soul consumed during the event is lost forever. Once the crisis ends, the Remnant
emerges permanently weakened. Entire portions of its accumulated soul reserves may
vanish in a single activation.
For this reason, scholars often describe Remnant's Cry as the final scream of an obsession
that refuses to let go.
Some believe that the stronger the love, desire, or obsession behind a Remnant, the more
terrifying its Cry becomes. Others believe that every Remnant's Cry is simply proof that even
after death, some souls are still incapable of accepting loss.
